Ours for the Taking

You are the first baby born on Atema Six, your birth heralding a boom; within a few months, all the corridors are full of burbling and screaming, of sleep-hazed parents, then of the stomping of tiny feet on the reinforced glass, as you learn to walk in a gravity your bodies were not designed for.

Sometimes your parents, or the parents of the other children, when they think you are asleep, will ask themselves in hushed tones whether this is really the place to raise children. But this world is the only one you know, and if you were to leave - and perhaps some day you will - you will be disorientated, homesick. You can't articulate it yet, but you know that this world, the reinforced glass base above swirling blue and green gas, the tremours that shake you each time the extraction platform is raised and lowered, the smell of decontamination fluids that cling to every adult you know: these, these are your home.
